Gucci CEO to Step Down as Luxury Brand Targets Global Market Boom

Project information

  • Category: Other
  • Project date: July 19, 2023

The president and CEO of Gucci, Marco Bizzarri, is set to step down on September 23, marking a significant move in the luxury goods industry. The French parent company, Kering, which also owns renowned fashion houses Yves Saint Laurent and Balenciaga, aims to capitalize on the flourishing global luxury market.
Bizzarri's departure comes amid a leadership overhaul orchestrated by Kering. Jean-François Palus, Kering’s managing director, will temporarily assume the reins. Palus is entrusted with reviving Gucci's influence and momentum, while strengthening its teams and operations.
Gucci's pivotal role in Kering's revenue is evident, contributing to over half of the €20 billion ($22 billion) in revenue last year. This strategic transition is poised to align Gucci with the thriving luxury sector, propelled by China's economic resurgence post-pandemic.
Luxury stocks, including LVMH, Hermès International, and Christian Dior, have experienced substantial gains this year. Kering's share price has also risen, reflecting the growing trend in the luxury market.
As Kering embarks on this transformation, the global luxury market anticipates a reshaping of its dynamics, underlining the strategic significance of Gucci's leadership change.
