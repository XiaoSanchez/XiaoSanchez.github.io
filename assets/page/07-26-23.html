Coca-Cola Halts Price Increases in U.S. and Europe, Shifts Focus to Developing Markets

Project information

  • Category: Other
  • Project date: July 26, 2023

Coca-Cola has announced the cessation of price hikes in developed markets such as the U.S. and Europe for the remainder of the year. Despite a 10% increase in prices during the second quarter compared to the previous year, shifting consumer preferences towards private label bottled water and juices led to a 1% decline in U.S. unit case volume. This decision echoes PepsiCo's similar stance in February, where it refrained from further price hikes for beverages.
Coke's CEO, James Quincey, highlighted the trend of consumers seeking value and opting for discounted products. Consequently, the company plans to align price adjustments with inflation in developing markets, particularly in Latin America.
In contrast, PepsiCo has experienced steeper demand declines. Its North American beverage volume dropped 4.5% in the second quarter, with only Frito-Lay North America showing a 1% volume growth due to consistent snacking habits.
Despite the price halt, Coca-Cola reported better-than-expected earnings and revenue, leading to a modest less than 1% drop in its stock value during morning trading. This strategic shift emphasizes a market-aware approach as both companies respond to evolving consumer preferences.
